Albert Einstein

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world."

- Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). He is best known in popular culture for his mass-energy equivalence formula E=mcÂ² (which has been dubbed "the world's most famous equation"), as well as his iconic formula for the photoelectric effect.

Einstein received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 "for his services to Theoretical Physics, and especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect".

Einstein's contributions to science and philosophy have had a profound impact on the world, and he remains one of the most recognizable and iconic figures in history.
